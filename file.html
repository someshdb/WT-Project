Blood Donation

Why Blood Donation

We need over 6,000 blood donations every day to treat patients in need across the World.
Each year we need approximately 200,000 new donors, as some donors can no longer give blood.
Most people between the ages of 17-65 are able to give blood. The need is constant and your contribution is important for a healthy and reliable blood supply.

Quick Facts About the Blood Donation Process

Blood donation is a simple, four-step process: registration, medical history and mini-physical, donation and refreshments.
It is a safe process, and a sterile needle is used only once for each donor and then discarded.
The actual blood donation typically takes less than 10-12 minutes.
The entire process, from the time you arrive to the time you leave, takes about an hour and 15 minutes.
The average adult has about 10 pints of blood in his body. Roughly one pint is given during a donation.
